Universal Discrete-Domain Speech Restoration

Abstract

This paper proposes a universal discrete-domain speech restoration (UDSR) model. Unlike regression-based speech restoration models that directly predict raw speech continuous features, UDSR redefines speech restoration as a classification task, instead predicting the discrete tokens generated by the residual vector quantizer (RVQ) of a pre-trained neural speech codec. Specifically, UDSR starts with random tokens and sequentially predicts VQ tokens of the raw speech, conditioned on global features extracted from the distorted speech, and ultimately decodes the raw speech. Experimental results confirm that our proposed UDSR can restore speech degradation caused by various types of distortion, e.g., noise, reverberation, band-limiting, phase distortion and compression distortion, demonstrating superior universality compared to advanced regression-based speech restoration methods.
